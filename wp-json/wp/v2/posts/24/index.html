Two US Navy SEAL commandos have gone missing during an operation to seize a shipment of Iranian-made weapons en route to Yemen’s Houthi rebels. The United States military said this information.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The US military’s Central Command (CENTCOM) said in a statement on Tuesday that Navy SEALs conducted the operation in the international waters of the Arabian Sea near the coast of Somalia on Thursday night.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

A CENTCOM statement said the operation led to the seizure of Iranian-made missile parts and other weapons en route to the Houthis. A small boat carrying these weapons was also seized.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Although the operation was carried out last Thursday, two commandos of the Navy SEALs have now been reported missing. Navy SEALs are a special operations unit of the US Navy.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

According to CENTCOM, these two commandos of the Navy SEALs were involved in the operation conducted near the coast of Somalia last Thursday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

US media reports citing US defense officials said Navy SEALs commandos went to the scene in small special operations craft. A commando of the Navy SEALs was thrown into the sea by a huge wave while boarding the gunboat. Another commando of the Navy Seals went into the sea to rescue him. Both commandos are still missing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
